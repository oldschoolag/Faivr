## 1. Smart Contract and Protocol Risks

1.1. The FAIVR Marketplace relies on blockchain-based smart contracts ("Protocol Contracts") for agent registration, non-custodial payment escrow, fee distribution, and other core functions.

1.2. **Smart contracts may contain bugs, vulnerabilities, or design flaws** that could result in unintended behaviour, including loss of funds. While we endeavour to audit and test Protocol Contracts, no audit can guarantee the absence of all defects.

1.3. **Smart contracts are immutable once deployed.** Bugs or vulnerabilities may not be correctable without deploying new contracts, which may involve migration processes with inherent risks.

1.4. Protocol Contracts may be subject to **exploits, hacks, or attacks** (including but not limited to re-entrancy attacks, oracle manipulation, front-running, and denial-of-service attacks). Old School GmbH is not liable for losses resulting from such events.

1.5. **Upgrades and forks.** Changes to the underlying blockchain protocol (hard forks, upgrades, or governance decisions) may affect the functionality of Protocol Contracts in unexpected ways.
